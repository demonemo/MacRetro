Steve Jobs was a co-founder and CEO of Apple Inc., the company that created the Macintosh, iPod, iPhone and iPad. He also co-founded Pixar Animation Studios and was a board member of The Walt Disney Company. He was a pioneer of the personal computer revolution and a visionary leader in technology and media. He was born in 1955 and adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs. He dropped out of college and traveled to India to study Zen Buddhism. He met Steve Wozniak in 1971 and they started Apple in 1976. He left Apple in 1985 and founded NeXT Computer and invested in Pixar. He returned to Apple in 1996 and led its comeback with innovative products and services. He died of pancreatic cancer in 2011 at the age of 56.
