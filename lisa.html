Apple Lisa

The Apple Lisa was a personal computer introduced by Apple in 1983.
It was the first personal computer to have a graphical user interface (GUI) and a mouse, which allowed users to interact with the computer using a more intuitive, visual interface instead of typing commands.
The Lisa was also notable for its high price, which made it inaccessible to most consumers at the time.
Despite its innovations, the Lisa was ultimately a commercial failure, with poor sales leading to its discontinuation in 1986.
However, its technology and ideas influenced the development of future Apple products, including the Macintosh, which became a more successful and iconic personal computer.
Today, the Lisa is considered a collector's item and a relic of computer history.

Apple Lisa Specifications
CPU Motorola 68000 @ 5 MHz
RAM 1 MB (expandable to 2 MB)
Storage 5 MB, 10 MB or 20 MB proprietary hard drive
Display 12-inch black-and-white CRT display with 720x364 resolution
Input Keyboard and mouse
Operating System Apple Lisa OS 1, later upgraded to OS 2 and 3
Price at launch $9,995 (equivalent to about $26,000 today)
